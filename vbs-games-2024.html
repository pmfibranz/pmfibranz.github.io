Game 1 - True or False Tag

Prep

Materials

  • Cones

Setup

  • Set cones in rectangular area with a dividing line

Play

  • Form two teams. Instruct teams to line up on opposite sides of the center line facing each other. Identify one team as the "True" team and the other as the "False" team.
  • The boundary line behind a team is the goal line.
  • When the leader says a true statement, the True players will run for their goal while the False team tries to tag them (ex. The sky is blue. Truth comes from God, Satan tricked Adam and Eve. etc)
  • If the leader says a false statement, the False players will run for their goal as the True team tries to tag them. (ex. Grass is purple. Adam and Eve obeyed God. Truth can be different for different people, etc.)
  • If players are tagged, they must join the other team. The team with the most players at the end of the game wins.

Game 2 - Beach Ball Bandits

Prep

Materials

  • 10+ beach balls
  • 4 hula hoops

Setup

  • Create square playing field by placing one hula hoop at each corner
  • Place beach balls in the center of the field

Play

  • Form four teams
  • On "Go," the first players of each team must run to the center, grab a beach ball, and take it back to their team's hula hoop
  • Then the next player on the team runs to the center and does the same thing
  • When all the beach balls are gon from the center, players can begin to take beach balls (one at a time) from other teams' hula hoops
  • When time is up, the team with the most beach balls in it's hula hoop wins

Round time: 5min

Game 3 - Fly Fishing

Prep

Materials

  • 20-25 soft domed cones or disks
  • Boundary cones
  • Sturdy stool
  • 2 large buckets

Setup

  • Place a sturdy stool in the center of a playing field
  • Create a small boundary around the stool using cones

Play

  • Form two teams and designate a bucket for each team
  • Instruct kids to spread out around the play area without crossing the boundary line
  • Stand on the stool with a bundle of soft domed cones or disks
  • Explain to kids that you will throw the cones like a frisbee over the playing field
  • Players must try to catch the cones before they touch the ground
  • If a player catches a cone/disk, they can place it in their teams bucket
  • Once you have thrown all of the cones/disks, the team with the most cones in its bucket wins

For older kids assign point values to each cone/disk color. The team with the most points in their bucket wins

Game 1 - Ships In The Dark

Prep

  • Cones

  • Blindfolds

  • Setup a rectangular playing field with boundary markers. Clear the field of any obstacles.

Play

  • Choose one player to be the "Lighthouse"
  • Choose 5-10 players to be "Rocks" and allow them to spread out around the play areas
  • Guid the rest of the players to be come "Ships"
  • The Ships must close their eyes or wear a blindfold as they attempt to navigate their way through the playing fields tot he Lighthouse
  • If a ship bumps a Rock, the Ship must freeze at the Rock, making a swishing noise like water hitting a rock
  • As the ships attempt to find the Lighthouse, the Lighthouse must continually make a beeping noise to help guid the Ships to safety
  • The first Ship to successfully reach the Lighthouse becomes the new Lighthouse
  • Allow kids to switch roles and play more rounds as time allows

Make more interesting by having multiple players link arms and walking together forming a larger ship

Game 2 - Sailboat Race

Prep

Materials

  • Pool noodles
  • Straws (2 per child)
  • Small foam triangles (1 per child)
  • Hole punch
  • Markers
  • Kiddie pool with water

Setup

  • Cut pool noodles into 1 to 2 inch rings (1 per child)
  • Hole punch each foam triangle on the top an bottom to create holes for the straws to go through

Play

  • Give each kid a pool noodle piece, two straw and a foam triangle
  • Give kids a few minutes to decorate their foam triangles, which will act as the sails on their boats
  • Show kids how to build their sailboats. Guid one straw through both holes of the triangle, and then stick the straw into the pool noodle
  • Once sailboats are built, allow 3-4 kids to compete in a sailboat race across the kiddie pool by using their second straw to blow into, creating wind
  • The sailboat that reaches the opposite side of the pool first wins

Create bracket to allow kids to compete in a sailboat racing tournamentSince working with younger kids pre-make some or all of the boats

Game 3 - Tide Pool Twist Up

Prep

Materials

  • 5-10 lightweight playground balls
  • Cones

Setup

  • Create a square playing field

Play

  • Choose one player to be the "Thrower" in the center of the square
  • Instruct the other players to choose a corner of the square to stand in. The corners will be considered safe zones
  • When the Thrower calls, "Tide Pool Twist Up," the other players must run from one corner to another (whichever corner they want) without getting tagged by a playground ball
  • As the players are running, the Thrower will try to get players out by throwing the ball below the neck and tagging them with it
  • Players who are hit by a playground ball become Throwers
  • The last runner standing wins

Game 1 - Flip Flop Frenzy

Prep

Materials

  • Cones
  • 4 sets of 12 (1 color per set) flip-flops or cutouts

Setup

  • Write phrases from the them vers on the flop-flops. Each color of flip-flops should contain the complete verse.
  • Create a square playing field with boundary markers. Place the flip-flops in the center

Play

  • Form 2-4 teams. Instruct teams to lineup around the square (1 team per side). Assign each team a color.
  • The first kid on each team will run to the flip-flops, grab one of the correct color, run back to his team, and high-five the next player to do the same.
  • Once a team has all of its flip-flops, it must put the theme verse in the correct order. The team that completes this first wins.

Display the theme verse (Romans 12:2) on a poster board

Do not be conformed to this age, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind, so that you may discern what is the good, pleasing, and perfect will of God. - Romans 12:2 -

Game 2 - Sand Castle Competition

Prep

Materials

  • Sand
  • Shallow plastic tubs (1 per team) could substitute with tarps
  • Sandcastle supplies (shovels, buckets, tubs of water, etc.)
  • Water balloons
  • Cones

Setup

  • Fill plastic tubs with sand
  • Fill water balloons

Play

  • Form teams of 4-5 kids. Assign each team to a sand tub
  • Instruct teams to build the best and sturdiest sandcastle they can in about 5 minutes
  • When the time is up, explain that teams will now attempt to throw water balloons at the other teams' sandcastles. Create a boundary line at each sandcastle to indicate where kids should stand while throwing
  • The team whose sandcastle stays standing the longest wins.

Game 3 - Pool Noodle Pickup

Prep

Materials

  • Pool noodles cut in half (2 halves per team)
  • Ball pit balls (10+ each color, 1 color per team)
  • Baskets (1 per team)
  • Hula hoop

Setup

  • Create a square playing area by placing baskets in the corners and balls in the center. Place two pool noodles at each basket
  • Position the hula hoop in the center of the play area. Place all of the balls in the hula hoop ring

Play

  • Form four teams
  • Instruct teams to line up at their designated baskets. Inform them of their designated ball color
  • Guide the first play of each team to run to the center with her two pool noodles and pickup a ball using the pool noodles like giant crab claws. She must then run it back to her team's basket without dropping it
  • If the ball is dropped, she must pick it up where it falls and continue making her way to her teams basket
  • After the first player makes it back to her team, she must hand off the pool noodles to the next player
  • Continue play until time is up or all the balls of one color are gone from the center. The team with the most balls of it's color wins.

Game 1 - Beach Ball Blitz

Prep

Materials

  • Cones
  • 10+ beach balls

Setup

  • Create a circle using cones
  • Spread the beach balls out

Play

  • Instruct kids to stand around the circle
  • Choose 3-5 players to stand inside the circle
  • Players on the outside will throw the beach balls, trying to tag the players in the center. Players in the center will try to dodge the beach balls
  • If players in the circle are hit with a beach ball, they must join the outside
  • The last player in the circle wins
  • Choose new kids to be in the circle and continue playing

Bible/Life Application: It might have been discouraging to be in the circle. When people disagree with us, it can be discouraging. Some people say, "If you don't agree with me, you don't love me." Similar to our game, these people might feel like they're being attacked when someone disagrees with them, but God says to speak the truth in love. (See Ephesians 4:15)

But speaking the truth in love, let us grow in every way into him who is the head, Christ.
- Ephesians 4:15 -

Game 2 - Animal Limbo

Prep

Materials

  • Pool noodle

Setup

  • Two leaders hold up a pool noodle between them as a limbo bar. Hold the noodle at a height that all kids will need to bend to go under it, but do not set it too low

Play

  • Guid kids to line up facing the limbo bar
  • Call out: "Penguin" Children must go under the limbo bar moving as a penguin
  • Call out other animals: bear, octopus, deer, beaver, mountain goat, wolf, snake, raccoon, squirrel,etc.

Try first without lowering the bar between turns just change the method of moving

Game 3 - Beach Towel Bunch

Prep

Materials

  • 5-10 beach towels
  • Cones

Setup

  • Create a play area using boundary markers
  • Spread out the beach towels around the play area

Play

  • Instruct kids to spread out around the play area and move around, not touching the beach towels
  • After a few seconds, call out a number
  • Kids must quickly run to a beach towel and sit down on it in a group of that number. (ex. If you call out , "Three," kids must sit in groups of three on the towels)
  • If any kids do not successfully find a group of the number called out, they are out but may take turns calling out the next number
  • Continue play as time allows, taking away beach towels as the remaining kids playing becomes fewer

Add music and pause then call out numbers.
Maybe try: Instead of eliminating players who don't find a group of the correct number, challenge them to complete a task, such as do 6 jumping jacks, give 4 people high-fives, etc. before playing the next round.
